Illuminated Molecules

 
Screen Space Global Illumination
 
 

Our screen-space global illumination (SSGI) provides high-quality, natural-looking lighting for any molecular data, enhancing depth perception and material cues beyond what direct lighting or ambient occlusion alone can achieve. By approximating indirect light bounces in screen space, SSGI captures soft shading in cavities, gentle color bleeding between nearby surfaces, and more balanced overall illumination, improving visual interpretability across proteins, complexes, and mesoscale assemblies.

To balance quality and interactivity, samples are traced iteratively: early results appear noisy but converge as additional samples accumulate. Adaptive denoising reduces this noise by applying stronger smoothing when only a few samples are present and tapering the effect as convergence improves. The renderer also adjusts the per-frame sample count automatically based on prior render time, maintaining responsive interaction while optimizing quality across a wide range of GPU capabilities.

Look for the icon in the viewport to toggle illumination mode and SSGI.

 
 

Protein–ligand scene showing PDB entry 6AU3. Left: The plain rendering appears flat, with minimal depth cues and limited separation between the protein surface and bound ligand. Center: SSAO improves contrast by accentuating cavities and contact regions, but the resulting shading is often overly dark and can obscure fine details. Right: SSGI produces a more balanced illumination, with indirect light bouncing across surfaces to yield a natural looking appearance that is easier to interpret visually.
